# Screening Platform Guide for Neurodivergent Job Seekers

**Last Updated:** January 31, 2026

This guide helps neurodivergent professionals understand what to expect from different hiring assessment platforms and which ones are more accommodating of different cognitive processing styles.

## General Strategies

### Before Any Assessment

**1. Request accommodations if needed:**
- Extra time for processing
- Asynchronous instead of live format
- Alternative demonstration of skills
- Written vs. verbal communication

**2. Know your rights:**
- ADA covers many neurodivergent conditions
- You can request reasonable accommodations
- You don't have to disclose your diagnosis
- Document all accommodation requests in writing

**3. Evaluate the company:**
- Refusal to accommodate is a red flag
- This shows how they'll treat you as an employee

### During Assessments

- Focus on demonstrating understanding over completion
- It's okay to say "I need a moment to think"
- Ask clarifying questions
- Comment your code/work thoroughly

### After Assessments

- Follow up with additional examples of your work
- Offer alternative demonstrations of skills
- Request feedback (though you often won't get it)

## Alternative Approaches

If a platform doesn't work for you, propose alternatives:

**Instead of timed algorithm tests:**
- "Could I complete a project demonstrating real-world skills?"
- "May I share my GitHub portfolio?"
- "Would you consider a pair programming session?"

**Instead of live coding:**
- "I work better asynchronously - could I submit a take-home challenge?"
- "Could I code privately and explain my approach afterward?"

**Instead of video interviews:**
- "Could we do a phone call instead?"
- "May I provide written responses?"
- "Would you consider a portfolio review with Q&A?"

---

## Red Flags

**Reconsider companies that:**
- Refuse any accommodations
- Use AI facial/emotion analysis despite concerns
- Insist on single assessment method
- Won't explain what they're measuring
- Claim their process is "perfectly objective"

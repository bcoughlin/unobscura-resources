# Pymetrics

**Category:** General Assessment  
**Format:** Neuroscience-based cognitive and behavioral games  
**Assessment:** ⚠️ Significant Challenges

---

## What They Do

Pymetrics uses 20-30 mini-games (taking 25-30 minutes total) to assess cognitive and emotional traits like:
- Attention
- Risk tolerance  
- Decision making
- Emotion recognition
- Processing speed
- Pattern recognition

Your results are compared to an "ideal profile" for the role.

---

## Why This Is Challenging

**Timed Games:**
- All games have time limits
- Processing speed heavily weighted
- Methodical, careful thinkers penalized
- Fast ≠ good, but system assumes it does

**Trait Matching:**
- Assumes one "right" cognitive profile per role
- Neurodivergent processing styles may not match template
- Cognitive diversity seen as mismatch
- No accommodation for different approaches

**Pattern Recognition:**
- System learns from historical hiring data
- If company hasn't hired neurodivergent people before, you won't match
- Perpetuates existing homogeneity
- "Fit" = conformity to past hires

**Emotional Recognition:**
- Games include reading facial expressions
- Disadvantages many autistic people
- Alexithymia not accommodated
- Different ≠ deficient

---

## What to Expect

**Format:**
- 20-30 mini-games
- 25-30 minutes total
- Must complete in one session (no breaks)
- Can't go back or review

**Types of Games:**
- Balloon pumping (risk assessment)
- Tower building (planning)
- Card games (learning/adaptation)
- Face reading (emotion recognition)
- Money exchange (fairness/trust)
- Digit memory (working memory)

**Evaluation:**
- Scored on speed and accuracy
- Compared to "successful employees" in role
- Algorithmic matching to profile
- Often used as initial filter

---

## Specific Challenges for Neurodivergent Candidates

**For Autistic Candidates:**
- Emotion recognition games directly disadvantage
- Alexithymia not considered
- Different risk assessment patterns
- Social cognition games biased
- Need for pattern understanding vs. quick reaction

**For ADHD Candidates:**
- Sustained attention games during hypofocus
- Impulsivity measures don't account for context
- Task switching difficulty across mini-games
- Processing speed variability not considered

**For All Neurodivergent:**
- Assumes consistent performance (not everyone's reality)
- Doesn't measure actual job skills
- Accommodations limited or unavailable
- Results used to filter before human review

---

## Pymetrics' Stated Approach

**They claim:**
- "Bias-reducing" technology
- "Scientifically validated"
- "Measures potential, not background"
- "Focuses on cognitive traits, not demographics"

**The problem:**
- Timed games still favor processing speed
- Cognitive traits are measured in neurotypical-normed ways
- "Ideal profiles" based on past hires (if homogeneous, perpetuates)
- Speed-based assessments discriminate regardless of intent

**To their credit:**
- More research-oriented than most
- Positioning as "fair hiring" tool
- May be receptive to neurodiversity feedback
- Better than pure resume screening

## Accommodation Strategies

**BEFORE the assessment:**

Contact company HR:

"I have [condition] that affects processing speed and [specific trait measured]. Pymetrics' timed games don't accommodate neurodivergent cognitive processing. I request:

1. Untimed version of assessments
2. Explanation of traits measured and job relevance
3. Alternative skills-based assessment
4. Ability to demonstrate qualifications differently"

**Questions to ask:**

"What cognitive profile are you matching against?"

"How was this profile determined?"

"What evidence links these games to job performance?"

"How do you account for neurodiversity in your matching?"

**If they won't accommodate:**
- This is information about company culture
- Proceed knowing you're at systematic disadvantage
- Consider other opportunities

---

## Alternative Approaches to Propose

"Instead of Pymetrics, could I:
- Complete a work simulation or job sample?
- Demonstrate skills through a trial project?
- Provide portfolio showing actual work quality?
- Do a skills-based technical assessment?"

**Frame as:**
"I want to show my actual capabilities for this role, which cognitive games don't capture accurately."

---

## Tips If You Must Take It

**Understand:**
- This measures pattern-matching to past hires
- Not a measure of your actual potential
- Fast ≠ good (but system thinks so)
- The tool has limitations

**During:**
- Read instructions carefully
- Answer honestly (faking rarely works)
- Don't overthink
- Remember: one data point, not your worth

**After:**
- Follow up emphasizing actual job skills
- Offer additional demonstration of capabilities
- Don't internalize rejection based on games

---

## Our Position

Pymetrics is **better than some alternatives** (HireVue, pure resume screening) because:
- Research-oriented approach
- Attempting to reduce certain biases
- Less obviously discriminatory than facial analysis

But still problematic because:
- Timed = processing speed discrimination
- Pattern matching = perpetuates homogeneity
- Cognitive trait assumptions may not account for neurodiversity
- Games may not predict job performance

**Most aligned of the general assessment platforms, but still has significant blind spots.**

---

## Bottom Line

If company uses Pymetrics:
1. Request untimed accommodation
2. Ask about trait matching methodology
3. Offer alternative demonstration of skills
4. If refused, assess company's accommodation culture

**Better than some, still problematic. Advocate for yourself.**

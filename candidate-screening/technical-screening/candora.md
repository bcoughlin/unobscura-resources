# Candora

**Category:** Technical Screening  
**Format:** AI-conducted technical interviews  
**Assessment:** 🔴 Proceed with Caution

---

## What They Do

Candora uses an AI interviewer named "Atlas" to conduct real-time technical interviews. The AI asks questions, evaluates responses, and scores candidates on coding, system design, and behavioral questions.

---

## Why This Is Particularly Problematic

**AI Can't Adapt:**
- Automated system can't adjust to different communication styles
- No human flexibility or judgment
- Rigid evaluation based on training data
- "Consistent" may mean consistently biased

**Training Data Concerns:**
- AI trained on historical hiring patterns
- If historical hiring excluded neurodivergent people, AI will too
- Learns what "good engineers" look like from biased data
- Perpetuates systematic exclusion at scale

**Communication Style Bias:**
- AI evaluates "typical" engineer communication patterns
- Literal, direct communication may be flagged
- Different explanation styles penalized
- No accommodation for processing differences

**Scale Amplifies Harm:**
- Can conduct "hundreds of interviews simultaneously"
- Means hundreds of false negatives if biased
- No human oversight to catch mistakes
- Faster doesn't mean better

---

## What to Expect

**Format:**
- Real-time interview with AI (not human)
- Voice interaction with "Atlas"
- Coding, system design, and behavioral questions
- Automated scoring

**Evaluation:**
- Technical correctness
- Problem-solving approach
- Communication style and clarity
- System design thinking
- "Team dynamic fit" (behavioral)

**Types of Questions:**
- Coding challenges
- System architecture discussions
- Behavioral/situational questions
- Project building exercises

---

## Specific Concerns for Neurodivergent Candidates

**Real-Time Pressure:**
- AI interviewer still creates observation anxiety
- No breaks or processing time
- Must perform in real-time

**"Think Aloud" Required:**
- Need to explain while coding
- Unnatural for many neurodivergent developers
- Bottom-up thinkers need more time to verbalize
- AI may not understand different explanation styles

**No Human Override:**
- No one to advocate for you if AI misses competence
- Can't explain "I work differently but produce good code"
- Systematic filtering with no recourse

**Behavioral Assessment by AI:**
- AI analyzing "team dynamic fit"
- Likely trained on neurotypical communication patterns
- Autistic communication style may be flagged
- Reinforces conformity over competence

---

## Critical Questions to Ask

**Before taking this assessment:**

"What data was the AI trained on?"
- Historical hiring decisions?
- What diversity was in training data?
- How was bias tested?

"Can I request a human interviewer instead?"
- This is a reasonable accommodation
- AI can't accommodate different processing styles

"How does the AI account for different communication styles?"
- Does it only recognize neurotypical patterns?
- Has it been tested with neurodivergent candidates?

"Can the AI's decision be reviewed by a human?"
- Or is it fully automated screening?

---

## Accommodation Strategies

**STRONGLY REQUEST:**

"I'm autistic/have ADHD and AI systems often can't accommodate different cognitive processing styles. Can I:
- Have a human interviewer instead?
- Complete an asynchronous project-based assessment?
- Have a human review the AI's evaluation?"

**This is a legitimate ADA accommodation request.**

**If they insist on AI:**
- Document your request for accommodations
- Consider whether this company values neurodiversity
- Proceed knowing the system may not recognize your competence

---

## Alternative Approaches to Propose

"I'm concerned that AI evaluation may not capture my actual technical abilities. Could we instead:
- Do a human-led technical interview?
- Review my GitHub portfolio with a real engineer?
- Complete a project that demonstrates real-world skills?
- Have a collaborative coding session?"

---

## Major Red Flags

**If company:**
- Refuses human interview alternative
- Can't explain how AI was trained
- Claims AI is "unbiased" (it isn't)
- Won't allow human review of AI decisions
- Uses this as sole screening method

**This indicates:**
- Automation prioritized over finding good candidates
- Likely won't accommodate you as employee
- Don't value diverse thinking styles

## Comparison to Other Platforms

**Candora is like:**
- HireVue (facial analysis AI) but for technical roles
- Codility but with no human in the loop
- The worst of automation + the worst of live interviews

**Why it's worse than human platforms:**
- Humans can adapt, AI can't
- Humans can recognize atypical excellence, AI follows patterns
- Humans can be trained on bias, AI inherits training data bias

---

## Our Position

Replacing human interviewers with AI:
- Trades human flexibility for algorithmic rigidity
- "Consistent" scoring means consistently biased if training data is biased
- Scales harm ("hundreds of interviews" = hundreds of false negatives)
- Removes accommodation possibilities

**Technical screening is already problematic for neurodivergent candidates. AI makes it worse.**

---

## Bottom Line

If a company uses Candora:
1. Request human interviewer as accommodation (ADA)
2. If refused, seriously reconsider this opportunity
3. This shows how they'll treat neurodivergent employees

**AI can't recognize competence expressed differently than its training data.**

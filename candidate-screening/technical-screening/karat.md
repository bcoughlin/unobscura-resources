# Karat

**Category:** Technical Screening  
**Format:** Human-led technical interviews (outsourced)  
**Assessment:** ✅ More Neurodivergent-Friendly

---

## What They Do

Karat provides professional interviewers who conduct technical interviews on behalf of companies. Unlike automated platforms, these are real humans conducting structured interviews.

---

## Why This Is Better

**Human Flexibility:**
- Real interviewers can adapt to different communication styles
- Can explain questions if unclear
- May pick up on competence even if expressed differently
- More accommodation possible than automated systems

**Professional Interviewers:**
- Trained specifically for technical interviewing
- Not your potential future manager (less personal stakes)
- Evaluated on quality, not just speed

---

## Still Challenging

**Live, Synchronous Format:**
- Real-time interview creates pressure
- Can't take breaks to process
- Time-limited (usually 60 minutes)

**Standardized Evaluation:**
- Interviewers use rubrics
- Communication style still matters
- May miss atypical demonstrations of skill

**Social Performance:**
- Still need to build rapport
- Cultural fit assessment may creep in
- "Soft skills" evaluation

---

## What to Expect

**Format:**
- Video or phone call with trained interviewer
- Coding problem(s) in shared editor
- Usually 60 minutes
- May include behavioral questions

**Evaluation:**
- Technical correctness
- Problem-solving approach
- Code quality and best practices
- Communication and collaboration

**Structure:**
- Introduction and warm-up
- Technical problem(s)
- Questions from you
- Wrap-up

---

## Accommodation Strategies

**Before the Interview:**

"I'm autistic/have ADHD and communicate differently - I'll demonstrate my technical skills clearly but may need accommodations:"
- Extra processing time
- Ability to think silently before speaking
- Clarification on ambiguous questions
- Written follow-up option

**During the Interview:**

"I need a moment to think through this."

"Could you clarify what you mean by [specific term]?"

"I'm going to write some comments first to organize my approach."

"I work better when I can research - in real work I'd look up [X]."

**If interviewer seems inflexible:**
- This may indicate company culture
- Consider whether this is where you want to work

---

## Alternative Approaches to Propose

"Could I complete a project-based assessment instead?"

"Would you consider a pair programming session where we collaborate?"

"May I submit a take-home challenge and discuss my approach?"

---

## Tips for Success

**Leverage the Human Element:**
- Ask questions - interviewers can help
- Explain your thinking process in your way
- Request clarification liberally
- Be honest about your approach

**Remember:**
- Interviewers want you to succeed (makes them look good too)
- They're trained to help clarify, not trick you
- Asking questions shows engagement, not weakness

---

## Bottom Line

Better than automated platforms because humans can adapt. Still has live interview challenges, but interviewers can be coached to recognize competence across different communication styles.

If the company is serious about neurodivergent inclusion, they can train Karat interviewers specifically on accommodations.

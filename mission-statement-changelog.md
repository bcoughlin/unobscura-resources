## [Version 4.0 - January 25, 2026] - Neurotypical Understanding

### Major Changes

#### Core Principles
- **Renamed Core Principle #3:** "Both Sides Benefit" → **"Neurotypical Understanding Is Essential"**
- **Reframed:** Centers neurodivergent need for understanding rather than mutual benefit framing
- **Emphasized:** Self-awareness alone isn't sufficient for thriving
- **Added:** Allistic family members, colleagues, and institutions must meet ND people partway
- **Key insight:** Sustainable success requires recognition of different cognitive styles as legitimate rather than deficient

## [Version 3.0 - January 25, 2026] - Complete Umbrella Mission

### Major Changes

#### Organization Naming
- **Changed:** Organization name from "NeuroMesh" to **Unobscura**
- **Rationale:** "Making visible what's been obscured" better captures mission of revealing hidden competence

#### Voice & Tone (Professional Consistency)
- **Converted to third person throughout** for professional consistency and trustworthiness
- Changed "Our mission" → "Unobscura's mission"
- Changed "We work" → "Unobscura works"
- Changed "We approach" → "The organization approaches"
- Converted all second person "you/your" to third person "people/someone/their/one's"
- Removed casual language ("That's where...") for more formal, professional tone

#### Core Principles - Psychological Safety
- **Renamed Core Principle #4:** "Prevention Through Understanding" → **"Psychological Safety Through Understanding"**
- **Reframed focus:** From prevention to psychological safety and crisis avoidance
- **Removed defensive language:** "firmly supports" and adversarial "however"
- **Changed framing:** Neurotype awareness complements (not competes with) social services
- **Added key insight:** Services respond during/after crisis but can't answer "Why does this keep happening?"
- **Emphasized:** Without neurotype awareness for all parties, rehabilitation work becomes difficult/impossible

#### Theoretical Foundation
- **Changed:** Grounded in broader autistic-led scholarship rather than solely Double Empathy Problem
- **Added:** References to monotropism, participatory research frameworks
- **Added:** Explicit centering of autistic people as experts on autistic experience
- **Rationale:** More defensible, comprehensive, aligned with lived experience ethos

#### Universal Neurotype Awareness
- **Expanded:** Foundation section to emphasize self-acceptance and mental health
- **Added:** Internal dimension - understanding one's own neurotype for sustainable life-building
- **Added:** "Self-understanding replaces shame" framing
- **Added:** Connection between neurotype awareness and reaching potential
- **Emphasis:** Two transformative aspects - (1) understanding yourself, (2) understanding communication with others

#### Focus Areas Restructuring  
- **Changed:** Candidate Screening moved from standalone area to sub-focus under "Professional & Vocational Impact"
- **Added:** Career Development as second major sub-focus (performance evaluation, manager training, advancement)
- **Expanded:** Family & Relationships to explicitly mention allistic/ND partnerships and "different communication operating systems"
- **Expanded:** Community Building to include both internal work (self-acceptance) and external work (finding community)

#### Voice & Positioning
- **Added:** "Grounded in autistic-led scholarship and lived experience"
- **Added:** "Centers neurodivergent people as experts on their own experience"

## [Version 2.0 - Draft 2] - Internal Talent Development Expansion

### Major Changes

#### Scope Expansion
- **Expanded:** From hiring-only focus to full employment lifecycle
- **Added:** Internal talent development and career advancement
- **Added:** Recognition that companies already employ neurodivergent people (often unknowingly)
- **Added:** Performance review and promotion barrier frameworks

#### Core Principles
- **Added:** "Career Aspirations, Not Potential Extraction"
  - Emphasizes neurodivergent employees have career goals
  - Rejects framing around "unlocking potential" or extracting value
  - Positions employees as having agency and professional ambitions

- **Added:** "Universal Neurotype Awareness"
  - Everyone has a neurotype with unique cognitive strengths
  - Applies to both neurodivergent and allistic individuals

#### Strategic Priorities
- **Split:** Technologies section into External (Hiring) and Internal (Employee Development)
- **Added:** Internal tools: performance review frameworks, manager training, career pathway tools
- **Expanded:** Success metrics to include retention, internal mobility, career satisfaction

#### Target Audiences
- **Expanded:** Primary audience to include people managers and HR teams
- **Added:** Secondary audience of neurodivergent employees pursuing advancement
- **Added:** Universal audience developing cognitive diversity literacy

## [Version 1.0 - Original Draft] - Hiring Reform Focus

### Initial Structure

#### Mission Statement
- Focused primarily on hiring and candidate screening
- Introduced Double Empathy Problem as core framework
- Established mutual benefit framing (costs both neurodivergent candidates and companies)
- Positioned as systems-level technical solution

#### Core Principles
1. The Double Empathy Problem
2. Mutual Benefit
3. Systems-Level Solution
4. Dignity Over Commodification

**Explicit Rejections:**
- "Autism as competitive advantage" narratives
- Savant framing
- Charity framing
- Positioning neurodivergent talent as "cheap labor"

#### Strategic Priorities
- Foundation: Research/Theory (DEP framework, assessment effectiveness data)
- Technologies: Job Description Decoder, alternative assessments, screening tools
- Change: Market-driven adoption, industry standard shifts

#### Target Audiences
- Primary: Hiring managers and talent acquisition
- Secondary: Neurodivergent job seekers

#### Voice & Positioning
- Technical/systems thinking approach
- Data-driven and pragmatic
- Solutions-focused, not complaint-based
- "Here's what works better for everyone"

## Mission Tagline Versions

### v2.0 - January 25, 2026 (mission-tagline-2026-01-25-v2.md)
**Frustration/Potential-Focused**

"We believe that different doesn't mean broken. The gaps in our understanding of each other's cognitive styles create frustration, confusion, and mistrust—limiting our collective potential. Our mission is to bridge these gaps by bringing awareness to our neurotypes. We seek to unobscure the many variations of thinking and sensing to make humanity more harmonious and equitable."

**Key Changes:**
- Leads with values statement: "different doesn't mean broken"
- Emphasizes collective cost of neurotype blindness
- More direct/action-oriented language
- Focuses on bridging gaps rather than visual metaphors

### v1.0 - January 25, 2026 (mission-tagline-2026-01-25.md)
**Camera Obscura Metaphor**

"The world can appear upside down when seen through eyes of someone different than our own. Our mission is to reveal the wonder of the human mind by bringing awareness to our own neurotype and that of others. We seek to unobscure the many variations of thinking and sensing to make humanity more harmonious and equitable."

**Key Features:**
- Explains "Unobscura" name through camera obscura metaphor
- Poetic/visual language
- Emphasizes wonder and revelation
